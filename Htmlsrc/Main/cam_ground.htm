Camera ground mode

Use the Ground tab in the camera dialog to select a ground-based camera mode. Ground-based views place the camera at a fixed point relative to the surface of a planet. This is a good way to follow the launch of a rocket from a spectator's perspective, or view the final approach of a Shuttle from the control tower.

You can select a ground view either by picking one of the predefined positions under the Ground observer location field, defined by a celestial body, a location and a viewpoint, and clicking the Apply button. Note that predefined ground locations may not be available for all celestial bodies.

Or you can simply click the Current button. This maintains the current camera location, but switches to ground observer mode.

There are two different operation modes for the ground observer camera: If the Target lock option is ticked, the camera will always be pointed towards the current camera target. You can move the camera around the target, and move in or out, but the target remains in the centre of the simulation window.

If the target lock option is not ticked, the camera is in free mode. In this case, it can be both moved and rotated independent of the view target.

In both modes, the speed of movement can be changed by adjusting the Panning speed slider.

The ground-relative camera position and direction data are displayed in the Position/Direction field.

Ground camera movement

A. Target lock mode

  • To move the camera around the target, use Ctrl ← →.
  • To move the camera towards and away from the target, use Ctrl ↑ ↓.
  • To move the camera up and down, use PgUp and PgDn.

The camera can also be moved by holding the right mouse button and dragging the mouse.

B. Free mode

  • To move the camera left and right, use Ctrl ← →.
  • To move the camera forward and back, use Ctrl ↑ ↓.
  • To move the camera up and down, use PgUp and PgDn.
  • To rotate the camera left and right, use ← →.
  • To rotate the camare up and down, use ↑ ↓.

The camera can also be rotated by holding the right mouse button and dragging the mouse.
