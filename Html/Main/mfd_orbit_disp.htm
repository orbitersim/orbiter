Orbit MFD display

Move the mouse over the images to see a description of the different elements.

Orbit reference. The celestial body relative to which the orbital elements are calculated.
Frame of reference. This can be either the plane of the ecliptic, or the equator of the reference object.
Projection plane. This is the plane into which graphical orbit display is projected.
Descending node. This is the point at which the orbit passes through the reference plane from above.
Ascending node. This is the point at which the orbit passes through the reference plane from below.
Periapsis. (also: perigee for Earth orbits and perihel for solar orbits). The lowest point of the orbit.
Apoapsis. (also: apogee for Earth orbits and aphel for solar orbits). The highest point of the orbit.
G-field contribution.The fraction of the total gravitational force acting on your ship which is contributed by the selected orbit reference object.
Orbit focus.The focal point of the orbit, given by the centre of mass of the reference object.
Radius vector.The straight line pointing from the orbit focus to the current vessel position.
Line of nodes.The intersection of the orbital plane with the reference plane (ecliptic or equator).
Green elements refer to your own orbit, yellow elements to the orbit of the target object, and the grey circle represents the planet surface.

SMa: Semi-major axis. The longest semi-diameter of the orbital ellipse. For nonperiodic orbits (e ≥ 1) this is the real semi-axis from the intersection of the hyperbolic asymptotes to the periapsis (displayed negative).
SMi: Semi-minor axis. The shortest semi-diameter of the orbital ellipse. For nonperiodic orbits (e ≥ 1) this is the imaginary semi-axis b = a sqrt(e2-1).
PeR: Periapsis radius. The distance between the orbit focus and the periapsis.

or

PeA: Periapsis altitude. The altitude of periapsis above the mean planet radius.

ApR: Apoapsis distance. Only applicable to closed orbits (e<1). The distance between the orbit focus and the apoapsis.

or

ApA: Apoapsis altitude. The altitude of apoapsis above the mean planet radius.

Rad: Radius distance. The distance between the orbit focus and the current vessel position.

or

Alt: Altitude. The altitude of the current vessel position above the mean planet radius.

Ecc: Eccentricity. Defines the shape of the orbit.

e=0: circular orbit
0<e<1: elliptic orbit
e=1: parabolic orbit
e>1: hyperbolic orbit

T: orbit period.Time for completing a full orbit. Only applicable to closed orbits (e<1).
PeT: time to periapsis.Time to the next periapsis passage. For nonperiodic orbits (e>1) this is negative when the object has passed periapsis.
ApT: time to apoapsis.Time to the next apoapsis passage. Only applicable to closed orbits (e<1).
Vel: orbital velocity.Current vessel velocity relative to orbit focus.
Inc: inclination.The inclination of the orbital plane against the reference plane (ecliptic or equator).
LAN: longitude of ascending node.The angle between the reference direction (e.g. vernal equinox) and the ascending node.
LPe: longitude of periapsis.The sum of the longitude of the ascending node and the argument of periapsis.
AgP: argument of periapsis.The angle between periapsis and the ascending node.
TrA: true anomaly.The angle between periapsis and the current vessel position.
TrL: true longitude.The sum of the longitude of periapsis and the true anomaly.
MnA: mean anomaly.The angle between periapsis and a hypothetical vessel position under the assumption of a circular orbit with the same period as the true orbit.
MnL: mean longitude.The sum of the longitude of periapsis and the mean anomaly.

Units: meter, second, degree
Suffixes: k: kilo, M: mega, G: giga, AU: astronomical units (mean radius of Earth orbit)
