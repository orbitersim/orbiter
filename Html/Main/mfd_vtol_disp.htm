VOR/VTOL MFD display

Move the mouse over the image to see a description of the different elements.

Telemetry source identifier for VOR or VTOL signal.
DIST: Distance from VOR/VTOL transmitter [m]
DIR: Relative direction of VOR/VTOL transmitter [deg] (0=ahead)
HSPD: Horizontal component of vessel ground speed [m/s]
Slaved NAV receiver.
NAV receiver frequency [kHz].
ALT: Altitude readout [m]
VSPD: Vertical component of vessel ground speed [m/s]
Altitude bar (logarithmic scale). Range: 1m - 10km.
Vertical speed bar (logarithmic scale). Range 0.1m/s - 1km/s. A green bar indicates positive vspeed, a yellow bar negative ground speed. A red bar is a surface impact warning.
Graphical display for vessel-relative position of NAV transmitter target and relative ground speed.
Relative ground speed indicator. Radial scale is logarithmic. Range 0.1m/s - 1km/s.
Relative location of signal transmitter. Radial scale is logarithmic. Range 1m - 10km.
Origin: current vessel position.
Legend:
Green/red circle: VTOL cone. This circle indicates the admissable deviation from the vertical touchdown vector as a function of altitude. During VTOL landing, the target indicator must remain inside the VTOL cone. a red circle indicates that the ship is outside the cone. The VTOL cone is displayed only when the MFD is slaved to a VTOL transmitter.
